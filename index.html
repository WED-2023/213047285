    • Ebrahim Raisi

    Ebrahim Raisi, an Iranian politician and Muslim jurist, served as the eighth president of Iran from 2021 until his death in 2024.

    He began his clerical studies at age 15 and held various positions in Iran's judicial system, including Prosecutor of Tehran. Raisi faced criticism for his role in the 1988 executions of Iranian political prisoners.

    He ran for president in 2017 and 2021, ultimately winning the latter election amidst allegations of rigging. Raisi's presidency was marked by strained relations with the U.S., intensified uranium enrichment, and support for Russia in its invasion of Ukraine.

    Considered a hardliner, he died in a helicopter crash in 2024.
